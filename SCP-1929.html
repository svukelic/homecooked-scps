WARNING: EXTREME LINGUISTIC HAZARD
NO VOCALIZATION ALLOWED
Item #:

SCP-1929

Object Class:

Keter

Special Containment Procedures:

SCP-1929 is not to be transcribed, recorded, or repeated under any circumstance. All audio and textual artifacts identified as SCP-1929 instances are to be incinerated or magnetically destroyed. Any personnel exposed to SCP-1929 phonemes are to be immediately quarantined and monitored for Phase One onset.

Research into counterlinguistic barriers is ongoing. All experimental language systems derived from SCP-1929 are suspended indefinitely. Exposure testing has been banned by order of O5 Command.

Instances of SCP-1929 discovered in digital archives are to be purged using embedded memetic filters. No attempts to reconstruct the language are to be made.

Description:

SCP-1929 is a language of unknown origin that cannot be traced to any known linguistic family. Exposure occurs through auditory, visual, or conceptual transmission. Subjects exposed to SCP-1929 undergo irreversible cognitive restructuring defined by progressive linguistic realignment and perceptual distortion.

Initial exposure (Phase One)

Manifests as spontaneous phoneme production during sleep or heightened emotional states. Subjects begin to write or speak symbols matching SCP-1929 structure without conscious intent. Transcriptions bear similarity to ancient scripts including Ge'ez, Linear A, and unknown logographic systems.

Phase Two

Includes partial comprehension of SCP-1929 syntax. Subjects describe visual overlays affecting spatial perception, identifying geometric "stress points" in people and objects. Communication attempts shift toward SCP-1929 vocabulary. Use of the language produces low-level environmental effects including minor structural warping and memory interference in nearby individuals.

Phase Three

Results in complete abandonment of native language and the use of SCP-1929 exclusively. Subjects exhibit changes in perception of time, gravity, and causality. Vocalizations in SCP-1929 at this stage cause spontaneous biological failure in listeners and localized reality degradation. Phase Three subjects are non-recoverable.

Linguistic analysis suggests SCP-1929 is not a descriptive language, but a functional framework—language as executable structure. Evidence supports the theory that SCP-1929 is the remnant consciousness of a pre-conceptual entity that once exerted direct influence on matter through pure expression. The language is not about the entity. The language is the entity.

Attempts to communicate with SCP-1929-speaking subjects result in auditory hallucinations and phrase mirroring. Listeners frequently report the sense that the phrase is "not being said, but remembered into them."

Addendum 1929-A: Incident Log Excerpt
Dr. ███████ accessed a fragmentary SCP-1929 glyph array via underwater archaeological tablet. Within six hours, subject had generated over 3,000 unique characters across all available surfaces, including self-inflicted inscriptions. Final audible phrase lasted 14 seconds and permanently disabled all nearby microphones. Translations failed. Physical space around the subject was later observed to flex inward by 3.2 cm for seven minutes before returning to baseline.

Addendum 1929-C: Incident Logs — Documented Exposures

Incident 1929-04: "Lexeme Echo"

Subject: Dr. Adrien C. Fleiss

Field: Forensic Phonology

Exposure Vector: Audio reconstruction from fragmented Sumerian tablet (Artifact-1929-Δ)

Time to Phase Two: 11 hours

Summary: Subject attempted manual extrapolation of SCP-1929 phonemes. Staff reported low-volume muttering from intercoms. Subject ceased English communication by Hour 7, began producing SCP-1929 root structures. Final written note: "I have re-learned cause from effect. The mouth follows the event." Subject terminated following vocal distortion event that ruptured internal microphones.

Incident 1929-07: "Scripted Wakefulness"

Subject: Agent Kalinda Rhee

Field: Linguistic Surveillance

Exposure Vector: Recovered cultist notebook

Time to Phase One: Immediate

Summary: Subject transcribed glyphs resembling SCP-1929 during decryption. Developed hypnopompic hallucinations and auditory perception of hidden language beneath all speech. Internal notes include: "I can see the logic behind my own thoughts. There's scaffolding under the grammar." Autopsy revealed looping optic nerve and cranial script formation embedded in cortex.

Incident 1929-13: "Child's Verse"

Subject: Noa V. (Age 9)

Exposure Vector: Spontaneous recitation during art project involving ceramic vessel (Artifact-1929-Kappa)

Time to Phase Three: 28 minutes

Summary: Subject vocalized rhythmic stanzas featuring SCP-1929 phonemes. Created 22 glyphs using sidewalk chalk. Exposed peers exhibited seizures and violent behavioral shifts. Subject neutralized after utterance triggered photic blackout and reversal of light source shadows.

Incident 1929-18: "The Double Mouth"

Subject: Prof. Leandro Taghavi

Field: Comparative Paleolinguistics

Exposure Vector: Echo resonance from SCP-1929-Lambda monolith

Time to Phase Two: 3 days

Summary: Subject described SCP-1929 as "not meant to be read, but spoken by something else using our mouths." Final transmission included overlapping audio from two vocal profiles, both identified as the subject. Autopsy revealed bifurcated tongue fused to jawbone, with glyphs etched into dental enamel.
