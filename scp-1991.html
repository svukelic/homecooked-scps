SCP

Item #: SCP-1991

The Regional Memetic Complex

Item #:

SCP-1991

Object Class:

Euclid

Special Containment Procedures:

Full containment of SCP-1991 is considered impractical at this time. Current containment strategy involves monitoring, documentation, and limited public awareness redirection. Affected geographical regions have been designated as Zone-Mosaic.

  • Foundation front organizations are to maintain cultural anthropology research centers in all Zone-Mosaic territories.
  • Foundation personnel embedded in media organizations are to promote narratives attributing SCP-1991-A manifestations to "colorful cultural traditions" and "regional temperament."
  • Zone-Mosaic borders are to be monitored for instances of SCP-1991 expansion.
  • SCP-1991-A instances who relocate outside Zone-Mosaic are to be monitored but not detained unless exhibiting Category 3 or higher manifestations.
  • Annual psychological evaluation of research staff assigned to SCP-1991 is mandatory due to observed memetic contamination risk.
Description:

SCP-1991 is a complex anomalous phenomenon affecting approximately 50 million individuals across a geographically contiguous region. Affected individuals are collectively designated as SCP-1991-A instances.

SCP-1991-A instances demonstrate consistent behavioral, cognitive, and physical anomalies that appear to be transmissible through both prolonged exposure and heredity. Current research suggests the anomaly has existed for at least 1,500 years, with historical records indicating consistent manifestation patterns despite significant geopolitical changes.

Primary manifestations of SCP-1991-A instances include but are not limited to:

  • Linguistic Anomaly: SCP-1991-A instances can understand each other at an approximate 65-80% comprehension rate despite speaking what linguistics experts classify as distinct languages. This mutual comprehension increases to 95-100% when discussing food, expressing frustration, or engaging in good-natured insults. When questioned, subjects will vehemently deny this ability while simultaneously demonstrating it.
  • Spatial-Temporal Distortion: Small gatherings (3-5 individuals) of SCP-1991-A instances will, with statistical improbability, transform into large celebrations (20+ individuals) within 2-3 hours. This is accompanied by the manifestation of elaborate food spreads that exceed the logically available resources. Analysis of video footage shows no clear moment of transition; the expansion appears to occur during brief camera cuts or moments of inattention by observers.
  • Memetic Resistance/Susceptibility Complex: SCP-1991-A instances display immunity to certain memetic and cognitohazardous effects, particularly those related to cultural homogenization. However, they simultaneously demonstrate extreme susceptibility to regional identity-based narratives and historical events, some dating back several centuries, which continue to trigger strong emotional responses despite temporal distance.
  • Competitive Hospitality Syndrome: When in the presence of guests, SCP-1991-A instances enter a state of compulsive generosity that can reach economically self-destructive levels. Attempts to refuse offered food or drink trigger distress responses in the host. In experimental settings, SCP-1991-A instances were capable of producing up to seven consecutive "final offerings" of food despite claiming each was absolutely the last.
  • Acoustic Anomaly: Music produced by SCP-1991-A instances, particularly using instruments designated as "traditional," has been observed to induce emotional states in listeners not consistent with the technical qualities of the music. Specific rhythmic patterns classified as "irregular" (typically in 7/8, 9/8, and 11/8 time signatures) trigger involuntary foot-tapping and hip movements in 85% of exposed subjects, regardless of cultural background.
  • Historical Hypermnesia: SCP-1991-A instances can recall with perfect clarity complex historical narratives dating back several centuries, despite demonstrating average or below-average memory function in controlled testing for non-regional information. These historical narratives often conflict between different affected groups but are maintained with unwavering certainty.
  • Gesture-Enhanced Communication: SCP-1991-A instances utilize hand gestures at a rate 300% higher than baseline human populations. Removal of the ability to gesture (through restraint or injury) reduces communication efficiency by approximately 72% and induces significant distress.
Addendum 1991-1: Notable Incidents

Incident 1991-A: During a Foundation-monitored cultural festival in Zone-Mosaic, three distinct groups of SCP-1991-A instances engaged in a heated debate regarding the proper preparation of a specific food item. Monitoring equipment recorded ambient temperature increases of 4.7°C in the immediate vicinity despite no additional heat sources. The debate concluded with all parties declaring victory simultaneously, followed by communal consumption of all disputed food items.

Incident 1991-B: Foundation researcher Dr. ███████ assigned to SCP-1991 for 3+ years began exhibiting SCP-1991-A manifestations despite no genetic connection to the region. Subject developed strong opinions about regional coffee preparation methods and began ending sentences with specific regional linguistic particles. Subject has been reassigned to non-contact research positions.

Incident 1991-C: A community of approximately 230 SCP-1991-A instances relocated to [REDACTED], Australia, in the 1960s. Satellite imagery from 1975-present confirms the gradual transformation of the surrounding landscape to match Zone-Mosaic topography within a 3km radius, including the emergence of architectural features not constructed by conventional means. Local residents report perceiving the area as "always having been that way."

Addendum 1991-2: Research Notes
  • Exposure therapy using mainstream Western pop music has proven ineffective in reducing symptoms. SCP-1991-A instances either develop immediate distaste for the music or begin performing it with irregular rhythmic patterns and added vocal embellishments.
  • Attempts to introduce standardized fast-food chains within Zone-Mosaic have consistently failed despite market research indicating viability. Businesses report inexplicable equipment failures specifically affecting standardization processes.
  • Foundation researchers have noted that SCP-1991-A manifestations intensify during specific calendar periods, most notably during the summer months and between December 31st and January 14th, when temporal anomalies have been observed with SCP-1991-A instances appearing to celebrate New Year's twice.
Addendum 1991-3: Interview Log

Interview with SCP-1991-A-42, a 78-year-old affected individual with no previous Foundation contact:

Dr. Park: Thank you for participating in our cultural research project. Can you tell me about your relationship with neighboring communities?

SCP-1991-A-42: Ah, these people? [gestures broadly westward] We have no problems with them now. But you must understand, in 1389... [Subject proceeded to provide a 47-minute uninterrupted, emotionally charged account of a historical battle with precision typically expected from specialized historians]

Dr. Park: That's fascinating. Now, I notice you're able to communicate with individuals from [neighboring region] despite the language difference.

SCP-1991-A-42: Different? No, no. They are the ones who speak differently. They ruin our beautiful language. I cannot understand them at all. [Surveillance footage shows subject conversing fluently with referenced individuals 37 minutes prior to interview]

Dr. Park: I see. Can you explain why you offered me twelve different food items when I visited your home?

SCP-1991-A-42: Twelve? This is nothing! You are a guest! You honor us! [becoming visibly distressed] You did not eat much—are you sick? Is the food not good? My wife will prepare something else. You must eat!

[End of excerpt]

Addendum 1991-4: Containment Considerations

The Ethics Committee has determined that full containment of SCP-1991 would be both impractical and unnecessary as the anomaly appears stable and presents minimal risk to normalcy preservation efforts outside Zone-Mosaic. Current containment strategy focuses on monitoring for potential escalation of anomalous effects.

Dr. ██████████ has proposed classifying SCP-1991 as a potential Thaumiel-class asset, noting that affected communities have historically served as effective buffers against other anomalous threats attempting to enter [REDACTED] from [REDACTED]. This proposal remains under review.

Field Agent ████████ documented instances of SCP-1991-A manifestations becoming significantly more pronounced in areas with highest [REDACTED] activity, particularly the "Competitive Hospitality Syndrome" and "Historical Hypermnesia" effects.

The neutralization attempt failed due to ███████ ███████ ████████ and unintentionally strengthened certain SCP-1991-A manifestations by approximately ██%. Surviving documents indicate that [REDACTED] considered the neutralization attempt "successful" despite all evidence to the contrary. O5-█ has authorized limited [REDACTED] to prevent similar neutralization attempts, as computer modeling suggests a second attempt could result in a containment breach and regional destabilization.
